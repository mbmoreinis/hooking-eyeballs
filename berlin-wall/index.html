Splitting a city in half

10/4/2023

Richard Pitoniak

Imagine the city of Westfield Ma. Now imagine it with a wall running down the middle, splitting it into two sections. One section continues to thrive and the other begins to slowly get worse. No one is allowed to cross over the wall, and families and friends are split apart. This happened to the German city of Berlin on "Barbed wire sunday"(August 13th, 1961). Berlin is currently the capital of Germany, with a population of over 3.6 million people, but was at one point cut in half by the infamous Berlin wall.

The Berlin Wall

After World War II Berlin was split in half by the two remaining world powers, the United States and the Soviet Union, after both groups agreed that the country couldn't be trusted on its own. This split would last until a new German government was formed that was acceptable to both parties. The US took control of west Germany and the Soviet Union took the east. Later on Britain and France joined the US in occupying west Germany. Western Germany, controlled by the US, would go on to have the highest population of any German city during the cold war. Berlin and Germany remained cut in half for 28 years, until November 9th 1989.
