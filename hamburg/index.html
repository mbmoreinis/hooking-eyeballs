Hamburg - Here at the Waterfront...

October, 5, 2023 | by Mason Quinn

People visit port cities like New York, Venice and Amsterdam all the time. But Hamburg? Unfortunately some people may hear Hamburg and think I'm talking about a food you eat come the 4th of July, however Hamburg, Germany has such a rich history that I feel is overlooked. Besides, Hamburg has far more canals than cities like Venice... Canals help people connect with the environment, and you won't find a more perfect city that has such a unique kind of breathtaking environment to immerse yourself in than Hamburg, Germany.

Hamburg is a large city in northern Germany, it is one of the largest German cities with 1.8 million inhabitants. Hamburg is a hanseatic city that as it once was a participating of the hanseatic league, a group founded by northern germanic cities and some merchant groups that existed between the 12th and 19th centuries; there are 18 hanseatic cities in Germany. Hamburg Harbor is one of the biggest and largest harbors in Europe. As said before Hamburg has some of the most canals of any city in the world (the real record goes to Cape Coral, FL) - in fact there are over 2500 canals in Hamburg compared to 400 in Venice and 125 in Amsterdam. Hamburg also has the most bridges of any city in the world with over 2300 being the official count. The canals are typically used for tourism in present day, but were very important to the city in the past as they facilitated industrial growth. While Hamburg may have had a glorious and prestigious past that goes back centuries the sprawling atmosphere of the city hasn't faded one bit. It still is one of the most important cities in Germany, and it's future looks bright...

Top 3 Places to go in Hamburg:

  1. Miniatur Wunderland - A sprawling, minaiture version of the world.
  2. Schmidt Tivoli - Beautiful musical theater at the heart of St. Pauli, Hamburg's party district.
  3. Hafen Hamburg - The sprawling port of Hamburg, you can even take a ferry to Copenhagen from here, but why would you want to leave such a great city?

But Why Hamburg?

While there are many cities to tour in Germany none are quite like Hamburg. One of the most major differences between Hamburg and a city like Berlin is it's location on the coast. Hamburg also embraces it's traditional style far more than a city like Stuttgart, this may make Hamburg very attractive to someone who loves the architecture of old. These are just a few reasons why I think that the next time you are planning a field trip, consider Hamburg... the city's calls.
