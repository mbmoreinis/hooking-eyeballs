Gravity

What is gravity? Gravity is what keeps you from flying into space at any time. It's what is simply keeping you on the ground as you read this. It is invisible, you don't know that it is there and you definitely do not ever think about it but it is there and always has been.

What are the Laws of Gravity?

The three laws of gravity. The first law is an object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at constant speed and in a straight line unless acted on by an unbalanced force. This is also know as "Inertia". A good example of inertia is the motion of a kite when the wind changes . The second law of gravity is the acceleration of an object depends on the mass of the object and the amount of force applied, known as "momentum". What does this mean? In simpler terms, an example of this is an aircraft’s motion resulting from aerodynamic forces, aircraft weight, and thrust. Lastly the third law of motion is whenever one object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal and opposite on the first. This is known as "Action & Reaction". All that this means is the motion of a spinning ball, the air is deflected to one side, and the ball reacts by moving in the opposite direction.

Who discovered Gravity?

Isaac Newton also known as Sir Isaac Newton was famously known for discovering the three laws of gravity and motion shortly after graduating from Trinity College located in Cambridge, England. Isaac was an english physicist and mathematician who was the culminating figure of the Scientific Revolution of the 16th & 17th century.
