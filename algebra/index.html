Algebra (The Origin)

Octuber 16 by Momin

First let's see where did the word Algebra came from? It came from an Arabic book's title in which it was called al-jabr. It means "the reunion of broken parts." The full name of that book was "Ilm al-jabr wa l-muqābala" written by a persian mathematician named al Khwarizmi. .Al-Khwārizmī’s algebraic work embodied much of what was central to Islamic contributions. He declared that his book was intended to be of “practical” value, yet this definition hardly applies to its contents. In the first part of his book, al-Khwārizmī presented the procedures for solving six types of equations: squares equal roots, squares equal numbers, roots equal numbers, squares and roots equal numbers, squares and numbers equal roots, and roots and numbers equal squares.

Algebra is balancing of the equations also known as al-muqābala in Arabic. it is a unifying thread of almost all of mathematics

In today's age, Algebra is used a lot in many things especially in technoligical advances. Many programic languages that we use today are made of Algebra.
